Grammarly AI vs ProWritingAid

2025 Comparison: Which Writing Assistant Wins?
Writing COMPARISON Published May 5, 2025 · Updated May 10, 2025

Quick Verdict

Grammarly wins for everyday writing with its polished interface and real-time suggestions. ProWritingAid wins for long-form and creative writing with its deeper analysis and style reports. Choose Grammarly for speed and simplicity; choose ProWritingAid for depth and craft improvement.

What Are These Tools?

Grammarly is the world's most popular AI writing assistant, used by over 30 million people daily. It checks grammar, spelling, punctuation, clarity, and tone in real-time across virtually every platform. Its 2025 AI features include full-sentence rewrites, tone detection, and generative AI for drafting content.

ProWritingAid is a deeper, more analytical writing tool favored by novelists, academics, and serious writers. It offers 20+ detailed writing reports, style analysis, and suggestions that go far beyond basic grammar. It's less flashy than Grammarly but more thorough for craft-focused writing.

Our Testing Process

We tested both tools head-to-head for 3 weeks on identical documents:

  • Blog posts and marketing copy (short-form)
  • A 5,000-word essay (long-form academic writing)
  • Email sequences (professional communication)
  • A 10,000-word fiction excerpt (creative writing)
  • Technical documentation (specialized content)
  • Social media posts (casual, informal tone)

Grammar & Accuracy

Both tools catch grammar errors effectively, but with different approaches. Grammarly is faster and more aggressive with suggestions — it flags issues in real-time as you type. ProWritingAid is more conservative, offering fewer but more thoughtful suggestions.

"Grammarly is like having an editor looking over your shoulder. ProWritingAid is like having a writing coach who sits down with you after you're done. Both are valuable, but for different reasons."

For catching typos and basic grammar, Grammarly edges ahead. For nuanced style improvements and identifying patterns in your writing, ProWritingAid is superior.

Key Differences

Interface & Speed

Grammarly's interface is more polished and responsive. It integrates seamlessly with Google Docs, Gmail, Slack, and virtually every web app. ProWritingAid's browser extension is functional but slower, and its web editor is less intuitive.

Writing Reports

ProWritingAid's 20+ writing reports are its killer feature. These include style analysis, sentence length variation, overused words, readability scores, pacing checks, and dialogue tag analysis. Grammarly offers none of these — it focuses on correctness over craft.

AI Features

Grammarly's generative AI can draft emails, rewrite paragraphs, and adjust tone on command. ProWritingAid's AI features are more limited, focusing on rewriting suggestions rather than full content generation.

Pricing

Grammarly

  • Free: Basic grammar, spelling, punctuation
  • Premium: $12/month — clarity, tone, full-sentence rewrites
  • Business: $15/user/month — team features, style guides

ProWritingAid

  • Free: 500-word limit, basic suggestions
  • Premium: $10/month — unlimited words, all reports
  • Premium Pro: $12/month — includes AI rewrites

ProWritingAid is slightly cheaper, especially on annual plans where it drops to $8/month. Grammarly's free tier is more generous for casual users.

Which Should You Choose?

✅ Choose Grammarly If

  • You write emails, Slack messages, and short-form content
  • You want real-time corrections everywhere
  • You need AI drafting and tone adjustment
  • You value speed and simplicity over depth

✅ Choose ProWritingAid If

  • You write novels, essays, or long-form content
  • You want to improve your writing craft, not just fix errors
  • You value detailed style and readability reports
  • You're on a tighter budget

Final Verdict

This is genuinely a "both are great" situation. Grammarly is the better all-rounder for most professionals — it's faster, works everywhere, and its AI features are more advanced. ProWritingAid is the better choice for serious writers who want to improve their craft. Our recommendation: start with Grammarly's free tier for everyday writing, and add ProWritingAid Premium if you write long-form content regularly.
